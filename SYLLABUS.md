# BSc Blockchain, Crypto Economy & NFTs - Course Syllabus

## Course Information

**Course Title:** Blockchain, Crypto Economy & NFTs
**Level:** Bachelor of Science (BSc)
**Credits:** 6 ECTS
**Duration:** 12 weeks (4 lessons per week, 48 lessons total)
**Format:** Blended (Lectures + Hands-on Labs)
**Prerequisites:** Basic programming knowledge (Python or similar), Understanding of basic economic principles

## Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive exploration of blockchain technology, cryptocurrency economics, and non-fungible tokens (NFTs). Students will develop both theoretical understanding and practical skills in distributed ledger technology, smart contracts, decentralized finance (DeFi), and digital asset management. Through a combination of lectures, laboratory exercises, and a semester-long project, students will gain the technical and analytical capabilities needed to design, evaluate, and implement blockchain-based solutions.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Technical Understanding**
   - Explain the fundamental principles of distributed ledger technology and consensus mechanisms
   - Analyze the technical architecture of Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other blockchain platforms
   - Develop and deploy smart contracts using Solidity and related tools

2. **Economic & Financial Analysis**
   - Evaluate token economics models and their implications for digital economies
   - Analyze DeFi protocols and their mechanisms (AMMs, lending, stablecoins)
   - Assess the valuation and market dynamics of cryptocurrencies and NFTs

3. **Practical Skills**
   - Set up and use cryptocurrency wallets and blockchain explorers
   - Interact with smart contracts on testnets and mainnets
   - Deploy tokens and NFTs following standard protocols (ERC20, ERC721, ERC1155)
   - Analyze on-chain data and transaction patterns

4. **Critical Thinking**
   - Evaluate security risks and vulnerabilities in blockchain systems
   - Compare different consensus mechanisms and scaling solutions
   - Assess regulatory frameworks and compliance requirements
   - Analyze real-world case studies of blockchain implementations and failures

5. **Design & Implementation**
   - Design tokenomics models for specific use cases
   - Propose blockchain-based solutions for business problems
   - Evaluate trade-offs in system design (scalability, security, decentralization)

## Assessment Structure

### Overall Grading Breakdown
- **Semester Project:** 70%
- **Quizzes:** 10% (5 quizzes x 2% each)
- **Laboratory Exercises:** 15% (12 labs, best 10 count)
- **Class Participation:** 5%

### Semester Project (70%)
Students choose one of four project tracks and work in teams of 2-3:

#### Track 1: Token Economy Design
- Design a complete tokenomics model for a specific use case
- Implement ERC20 token with custom features
- Analyze distribution, incentives, and sustainability
- Deliverables: Whitepaper (15%), Smart Contract Code (25%), Economic Analysis (20%), Presentation (10%)

#### Track 2: NFT Platform Development
- Design and deploy an NFT collection with utility
- Implement minting, trading, and metadata management
- Analyze market positioning and user adoption strategy
- Deliverables: Technical Documentation (15%), Smart Contracts & Frontend (25%), Market Analysis (20%), Presentation (10%)

#### Track 3: DeFi Protocol Analysis
- Deep analysis of a DeFi protocol (TVL, risks, economics)
- Simulate protocol behavior under different market conditions
- Propose improvements or identify vulnerabilities
- Deliverables: Technical Report (15%), Simulation Code (25%), Risk Assessment (20%), Presentation (10%)

#### Track 4: Supply Chain Blockchain Solution
- Design blockchain-based supply chain tracking system
- Implement proof-of-concept with smart contracts
- Analyze business case and ROI for real-world deployment
- Deliverables: System Design (15%), Implementation (25%), Business Case (20%), Presentation (10%)

**Project Milestones:**
- Week 3: Team formation & topic selection
- Week 6: Project proposal submission (ungraded, feedback only)
- Week 9: Interim presentation (feedback only)
- Week 12: Final submission & presentation

### Quizzes (10%)
- 5 quizzes administered in weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 10
- 15-minute multiple choice and short answer format
- Covers material from previous 2 weeks
- Each quiz worth 2% of final grade

### Laboratory Exercises (15%)
- 12 hands-on labs throughout the semester
- Submit lab report within 1 week of completion
- Best 10 out of 12 labs count toward grade (1.5% each)
- Focus on practical skills: wallet setup, smart contract deployment, DeFi interaction, security analysis

### Class Participation (5%)
- Active engagement in discussions
- Contribution to peer learning
- Quality of questions and insights
